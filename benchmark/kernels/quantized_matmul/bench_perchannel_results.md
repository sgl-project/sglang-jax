| Large N, small K | 8192x1024, 4096x512 | **2-8.7x** | **2.2-4.8x** | **7.3-8.7x** | 0.1-0.28x |
| Large N, large K | 4096x8192, 8192x4096 | 0.65-0.80x | 0.68-0.86x | 0.03x | 0.03-0.04x |

### Key findings

1. **3rd-party kernel wins decisively for small/medium matrices** (N or K <= 2048): consistent 4-6x speedup across all batch sizes for both INT8 and FP8.

2. **INT8 is consistently better than FP8 on the 3rd-party kernel**, especially at large batch: INT8 maintains speedup at batch 8K-16K for most shapes, while FP8 degrades badly. The int32 accumulator in the 3rd-party kernel is the key advantage for INT8.

3. **Large matrices (both N>=4096 and K>=4096) are slower on the 3rd-party kernel**: the Pallas kernel lacks tuned block sizes for these shapes on TPU v6, causing severe performance degradation especially at large batch (0.03x = 30x slower).

4. **Accuracy**: rel_err is ~0.3% for INT8 and ~2% for FP8 between the two implementations. Both are within acceptable bounds — the numerical difference comes from accumulation order and precision (int32 vs float32).

5. **Recommendation**: Use 3rd-party kernel for shapes where `min(N, K) <= 2048` or where the matrix is not square-large. For large square matrices, the JAX fallback is significantly faster until tuned block sizes are added for those shapes.
